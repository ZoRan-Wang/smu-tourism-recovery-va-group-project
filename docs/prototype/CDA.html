Singapore Tourism Recovery Visual Analytics CDA

Author

Xi Zixun

The exploratory data analysis (EDA) identified several key patterns in Singapore’s tourism data, including the sharp decline in visitor arrivals during the COVID-19 period and potential relationships between tourism indicators. Based on these observations, confirmatory data analysis (CDA) is conducted to statistically test these relationships.

Preperation

Within This analysis, a factor to be consider is periods. pre_covid represents the year 2017 to 2019 (End). Covid_shock Represents 2020-2021 (End), recovery represents Year 2022 - 2025(End)

CDA (Confirmatory Data Analysis)

CDA 1 - Statistical Impact of COVID-19 on Visitor Arrivals

Did the COVID-19 pandemic significantly affect visitor arrivals to Singapore?

Hypothesis

H₀ (Null Hypothesis) There is no significant difference in visitor arrivals across the tourism periods.

H₁ (Alternative Hypothesis) Visitor arrivals differ significantly across the tourism periods.

plot_ly(
  data = data_clean,
  x = ~period,
  y = ~visitor_arrivals,
  color = ~period,
  type = "box",
  boxpoints = "outliers"
) %>%
  layout(
    title = "Visitor Arrivals Across Tourism Periods",
    xaxis = list(title = "Period"),
    yaxis = list(title = "Visitor Arrivals (Millions)"),
    showlegend = FALSE
  )
anova_model <- aov(visitor_arrivals ~ period, data = data_clean)

Note

The boxplot compares the distribution of visitor arrivals across different tourism periods. A substantial decline in the median visitor arrivals is observed during the COVID-19 period compared to the pre-COVID period, indicating a significant disruption to tourism demand. The interquartile range also shifts downward during the pandemic, suggesting that the overall level of visitor arrivals was consistently lower across most observations. In contrast, the recovery period shows an upward shift in the distribution, reflecting the gradual rebound of tourism activity after travel restrictions were lifted. Overall, the distributional differences across periods support the conclusion that the COVID-19 pandemic had a significant impact on Singapore’s tourism sector.

CDA 2 - Influence of Chinese Visitor Share on Total Visitor Arrivals

Does the share of Chinese visitors significantly influence total visitor arrivals to Singapore?

Hypothesis

H₀ (Null Hypothesis) China visitor share has no significant relationship with total visitor arrivals.

H₁ (Alternative Hypothesis) China visitor share significantly influences total visitor arrivals.

CDA 3 - Seasonal Variation in Visitor Arrivals

Do visitor arrivals vary significantly across different months?

Hypothesis

H₀ (Null Hypothesis) Visitor arrivals do not differ significantly across months.

H₁ (Alternative Hypothesis) Visitor arrivals vary significantly across months, indicating seasonal tourism patterns.

data_clean <- data_clean %>%
  mutate(month = month(date, label = TRUE, abbr = TRUE))
data_no_covid <- data_clean %>%
  filter(!(year %in% c(2020, 2021)))
p <- ggplot(
  data_no_covid,
  aes(
    x = month,
    y = visitor_arrivals,
    text = paste(
      "Month:", month,
      "<br>Visitor Arrivals:", format(visitor_arrivals, big.mark = ",")
    )
  )
) +
  geom_boxplot(fill = "skyblue") +
  labs(
    title = "Seasonal Variation in Visitor Arrivals",
    x = "Month",
    y = "Visitor Arrivals"
  ) +
  theme_minimal()

ggplotly(p, tooltip = "text")
anova_month <- aov(visitor_arrivals ~ month, data = data_no_covid)

summary(anova_month)

Tip

  • The monthly distributions are based on observations from 2017–2025, with the COVID shock years (2020–2021) excluded to avoid distortion in seasonal patterns.

  • Pr(>F): Probability of observing an F value greater than the calculated F-statistic under the null hypothesis. (It is the p-value associated with the F-statistic.)

A one-way ANOVA test was conducted to examine whether visitor arrivals differ significantly across months. The analysis was performed using observations from 2017–2025, excluding the COVID shock years (2020–2021) to avoid distortion in seasonal patterns. The boxplot illustrates the distribution of visitor arrivals for each month, allowing potential seasonal variation to be visually assessed. However, the ANOVA results indicate that the effect of month on visitor arrivals is not statistically significant (F = 0.525, p = 0.881). Since the p-value is greater than the significance level of 0.05, the null hypothesis cannot be rejected. This suggests that visitor arrivals do not vary significantly across months in the dataset, indicating limited evidence of strong seasonal patterns in tourism demand during the analyzed period.
