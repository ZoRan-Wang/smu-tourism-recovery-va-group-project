Prototype: Clustering Analysis

Monthly tourism market states in Singapore’s recovery

Module Focus

This prototype tests whether monthly observations can be grouped into a small number of tourism market states during Singapore’s pandemic shock and recovery.

The analytical unit is the month, not the individual traveller. The module therefore asks a market-state question:

Do months naturally separate into recurring states defined by visitor volume, China’s market share, hotel occupancy, and length of stay?

Draft Review And Corrections

The original draft had the right direction, but it needed three corrections before it could become a valid prototype module report.

  1. The clustering variable for stay duration should be avg_stay_monthly_capped, not the raw monthly series, because extreme pandemic values would otherwise dominate distance calculations.
  2. The method should be described as k-means on standardized monthly indicators, not copied too closely from survey-style latent class workflows.
  3. The choice of k = 3 should be justified as an interpretability decision supported by diagnostics, rather than treated as an unquestioned default.

Why This Prototype Uses K-Means

The structure of this page is inspired by the clustering workflow shown on the Habari Tanzania clustering analysis: prepare the data, fit the model, append class labels, and visualise the resulting groups.

However, the underlying algorithm is different for a good reason.

  • The Habari Tanzania page works with transformed categorical indicators and latent classes.
  • This tourism dataset contains continuous monthly market indicators and only 110 monthly observations.
  • For this case, standardized k-means is a better prototype because it is transparent, fast to test in Shiny, and easy to explain in a coursework setting.

Data Preparation For The Module

The clustering input uses the cleaned workbook tourism_four_part_analysis_ready.xlsx, covering 2016-12 to 2026-01.

The four modeling variables are:

  1. visitor_arrivals
  2. china_share
  3. hotel_occ
  4. avg_stay_monthly_capped

This design follows the project logic closely.

  • visitor_arrivals captures market size.
  • china_share captures the structural weight of the Chinese source market.
  • hotel_occ captures accommodation market recovery.
  • avg_stay_monthly_capped retains the stay signal without letting extreme pandemic months overpower the model.

Cluster Selection

The prototype checks candidate solutions from k = 2 to k = 6.

K Total within-cluster SS Mean silhouette
2 166.4 0.665
3 109.6 0.632
4 74.9 0.544
5 55.7 0.514
6 48.1 0.381
Important

k = 2 produces the highest mean silhouette (0.665), but the prototype keeps k = 3.

This is a deliberate modeling choice. The three-cluster solution separates:

  • a low-volume pandemic shock state,
  • a transitional recovery state,
  • and a high-performance market state.

That structure matches the substantive story of the project better than a simpler two-cluster split.

The three states are substantively interpretable.

  • Pandemic shock months have extremely low arrivals, low occupancy, and unusually long stays even after outlier treatment.
  • Recovery transition months still have subdued arrivals and occupancy, but they sit above the most disrupted months.
  • High-performance months dominate on visitor volume and hotel occupancy, with short-to-normal stay durations.

One especially important interpretation point is china_share. During the deepest shock months, China’s share can be high even when China’s absolute visitor count is low, because the overall market collapsed even more sharply. This is exactly why the report should present both volume and share as complementary signals.

This distribution is useful for storytelling in the final report.

  • The high-performance state covers all pre_covid months and most recovery months.
  • The pandemic shock state is concentrated entirely inside the covid_shock period.
  • The recovery transition state bridges the end of the shock period and the earliest recovery months.

That pattern supports the main claim of the module: months are not evenly scattered, but instead cluster into recurring tourism market conditions.

UI Design For The Shiny Module

This prototype will later become a Shiny module, so the report must define the exposed controls clearly.

Exposed Parameters

Parameter UI component Default Design note
period_filter checkboxGroupInput all three periods supports focused comparison
k_value sliderInput 3 allows quick sensitivity testing
random_seed numericInput 42 keeps clustering reproducible
run_model actionButton click to execute prevents unnecessary recomputation

Exposed Outputs

Output Format Why it matters

Timeline of assigned states ggplot2 links clusters back to historical phases
Quality metric value box or text card shows silhouette score for the selected run
Clustered month table downloadable CSV supports follow-up analysis outside the app

UI Storyboard Notes

  1. Keep the left panel for controls and the right panel for results.
  2. Preserve the same state labels and colors across every output.
  3. Keep standardization fixed on in the prototype app, because turning it off would create misleading distance behavior.
  4. Surface a short helper note explaining that china_share is a structural measure, not a raw visitor count.

Implementation Notes

The module specification now aligns with the current project structure.

  1. Data source: data/raw/tourism_four_part_analysis_ready.xlsx
  2. Current Shiny entry points: app/R/data_utils.R, app/R/mod_cluster_ui.R, app/R/mod_cluster_server.R
  3. Prototype baseline: monthly k-means with k = 3, seed 42, and standardized features

Prototype Conclusion

This clustering prototype supports a clear analytical message for the project:

Monthly tourism conditions in Singapore do not form one smooth continuum. They separate into interpretable market states that combine overall visitor volume, China’s market share, hotel occupancy, and length of stay.

That makes the module suitable for both the coursework report and a later interactive Shiny implementation.
